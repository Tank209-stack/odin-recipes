Chole Bhature

chole bhature delhi famous food

Ingredients

Chole
  • Chickpeas (Garbanzo beans)
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Green chilies
  • Cooking oil
  • Cumin seeds
  • Turmeric powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Garam masala
  • Amchur (dry mango powder) or Anardana (pomegranate seed powder)
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) for garnish
Bhature
  • All-purpose flour (Maida)
  • Semolina (Sooji/Rava)
  • Yogurt
  • Baking powder
  • Baking soda
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Cooking oil (for dough and frying)

Steps

Chole
  1. Soak chickpeas (garbanzo beans) overnight or for at least 6-8 hours.
  2. Boil the soaked chickpeas with salt until tender, then drain and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies paste, sauté until the raw smell goes away.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until oil separates.
  7. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for a minute.
  8. Add boiled chickpeas, salt, and water. Mix well, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  9. Add amchur (dry mango powder) or anardana (pomegranate seed powder) for tangy flavor.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) and serve hot.
bhature
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour (maida), semolina (sooji/rava), baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt and knead into a smooth dough, adding water gradually if needed.
  3. Apply a little oil on the dough, cover, and let it rest for 2-3 hours.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls, roll out each ball into a thin circle (bhatura shape).
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium-high heat.
  6. Gently slide the rolled bhatura into the hot oil, press lightly with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
  7. Fry until golden brown on both sides, then drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve hot bhature with delicious chole.
