Biryani

biryani Royal food of Nawabs

Ingredients

  • Chicken (½ kilogram, skinless bone-in or boneless, large pieces such as thighs, drumsticks, legs, or a mix of all)
  • Plain Yogurt (3 tablespoons)
  • Ginger Garlic Paste (1 ¼ tablespoons, made from 1-inch ginger and 4 garlic cloves crushed)
  • Salt (¾ teaspoon, adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric (¼ teaspoon)
  • Red Chilli Powder (1 teaspoon, or paprika; use less spicy variety)
  • Garam Masala (1 tablespoon, or biryani masala; refer to notes)
  • Lemon Juice (optional, 1 tablespoon)
  • Bay Leaf (1)
  • Green Cardamoms (4)
  • Cloves (6)
  • Cinnamon (1-inch stick)
  • Star Anise (1)
  • Shahi Jeera (¾ teaspoon, substitute with cumin)
  • Mace (1 strand, omit if you don’t like)
  • Basmati Rice (2 cups, aged rice only)
  • Ghee (or Oil, 2 tablespoons)
  • Large Onion (1, thinly sliced)
  • Mint Leaves (½ cup, finely chopped, about 15 leaves)
  • Green Chili (1, slit or chopped)
  • Plain Yogurt (¼ cup, Indian curd)
  • Red Chili Powder (optional, for heat, ½ teaspoon)
  • Water (3 cups, 3 ½ cups for pot, refer to notes, more if needed)
  • Fried Onions (optional, 2 tablespoons)
  • Saffron (optional, soaked in 2 tablespoons hot milk)

steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Mix chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices (like turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala), and lemon juice. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a saucepan, heat butter and sauté sliced onions, bay leaf, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick until onions are golden brown.
  3. Add Chicken: Add marinated chicken to the sautéed onions and cook until partially done.
  4. Par-Cook Rice: Rinse basmati rice and soak it in water. Drain and set aside. Boil water with whole spices (like cumin, cardamom, and cinnamon). Add rice and cook for a few minutes until partially done. Drain.
  5. Layer Chicken and Rice: Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the saucepan.
  6. Infuse Flavors: Drizzle saffron-infused milk, fried onions, and ghee (or attar) over the rice. Seal the pot with a lid to trap steam.
  7. Dum Cooking: Cook on low heat (dum) for tender and flavorful Chicken Biryani.
  8. Serve: Garnish with more fried onions and serve hot with raita or shorba.
